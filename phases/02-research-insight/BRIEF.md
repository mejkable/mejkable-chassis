# Phase 02 — Research & Insight: BRIEF

## Purpose

Build the knowledge base that informs every decision downstream. This phase answers: **"What do we need to know, and what did we find out?"**

Research here is targeted, not open-ended. The assumption inventory and open questions from Phase 01 set the agenda. The goal is to reduce uncertainty on the things that matter most before committing to a product direction.

---

## Inputs Needed

- Phase 01 HANDOVER.md — especially the top unvalidated assumptions and open questions
- Any existing research, data, or domain knowledge the team already has
- Budget/time constraints for research activities (this scopes ambition)

---

## Sub-Task Menu

### Core (recommended for all projects)

- [ ] **Competitive deep-dive** — Go beyond the Phase 01 scan. Detailed analysis of key competitors: products, pricing, positioning, strengths, weaknesses, reviews, market share.
- [ ] **User research synthesis** — Gather and synthesise user insights. This can range from reviewing existing data (forums, reviews, social media) to conducting interviews or surveys.
- [ ] **Market sizing & segmentation** — Quantify the opportunity. TAM/SAM/SOM or equivalent. Validate or refine the segment chosen in Phase 01.
- [ ] **Assumption validation tracker** — Systematically test the top assumptions from Phase 01. Record what was validated, invalidated, or remains uncertain.

### Conditional (select based on product type and situation)

- [ ] **Materials & technology scan** — What materials, components, or technologies are available? What's emerging? Cost/performance/availability trade-offs. (Critical for hardware, electronics, innovative products)
- [ ] **Manufacturing method exploration** — What manufacturing processes are feasible? Injection moulding, PCB assembly, print production, CNC, 3D printing, etc. First-pass on capabilities and constraints.
- [ ] **Supplier landscape mapping** — Who makes the components or can manufacture the product? Regions, MOQs, lead times, quality tiers.
- [ ] **Regulatory & compliance research** — Detailed requirements: safety standards, certifications, labelling, testing. What's mandatory vs. recommended? (e.g., CE, UL, CPSIA, EN71 for toys, battery regulations)
- [ ] **Patent & IP deep-dive** — Freedom-to-operate analysis. Existing patents in the space, design registrations, trademarks to avoid.
- [ ] **Channel & distribution research** — How does this product category reach customers? Retailer requirements, DTC logistics, marketplace dynamics, margin expectations.
- [ ] **Pricing & willingness-to-pay research** — Price benchmarking across competitors and substitutes. Price sensitivity signals from the market.
- [ ] **Trend & cultural context research** — Deep dive on relevant trends identified in Phase 00/01. Evidence base for timing and positioning decisions.

### Optional / Deep Dive

- [ ] **Expert consultation synthesis** — Capture insights from domain experts, manufacturers, retailers, or advisors.
- [ ] **Teardown analysis** — Physically or analytically tear down competitor products. BOM estimation, construction methods, quality assessment.
- [ ] **Supply chain risk assessment** — Single-source dependencies, geopolitical risks, lead time variability, shipping/tariff considerations.
- [ ] **Sustainability & environmental scan** — Material sourcing ethics, end-of-life considerations, eco-labelling requirements, consumer expectations around sustainability.
- [ ] **Adjacent category analysis** — Lessons from related product categories that could inform design, pricing, or go-to-market.
- [ ] **Investment case development** — If seeking external funding: compile market data, competitive landscape, unit economics, and growth projections into an investor-ready narrative. Build the evidence base for pitch materials, grant applications, or crowdfunding campaign copy.

---

## Done Criteria

This phase is complete when:

1. The top assumptions from Phase 01 have been tested and their status updated
2. The competitive landscape is understood well enough to differentiate
3. Key technical/manufacturing feasibility questions have first-pass answers
4. Enough market data exists to support product definition decisions in Phase 03
5. The HANDOVER.md provides a clear, evidence-based foundation for defining the product

---

## Notes

- Research can be a time sink. Set a time-box for this phase and focus on the highest-risk assumptions first. Perfect information doesn't exist — aim for "enough to decide."
- For solopreneurs, AI-assisted desk research gets you 70-80% of the way. The remaining 20% often requires conversations with real users, suppliers, or retailers. Don't skip those.
- Some research sub-tasks (materials, manufacturing, regulatory) may feel premature. They're not — discovering a regulatory blocker or manufacturing impossibility after you've designed the product is catastrophically expensive.
- Capture everything in WORKBOOK.md even if it seems tangential. Insights from this phase often become critical much later.
