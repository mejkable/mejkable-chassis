# Phase 09 — Post-Launch & Iteration: BRIEF

## Purpose

Monitor, learn, and improve after launch. This phase answers: **"How is the product performing, what are customers telling us, and what's next?"**

This is not a one-time phase — it's an ongoing operating mode. The product is live, customers are using it, and the feedback loop drives continuous improvement, inventory decisions, and next-version planning.

---

## Inputs Needed

- Phase 08 HANDOVER.md — launch details, success metrics, feedback channels, known risks
- Access to sales data, customer feedback, and analytics
- Phase 01-03 materials for reference when evaluating feedback against original intent

---

## Sub-Task Menu

### Core (recommended for all projects)

- [ ] **Performance monitoring** — Track key metrics: sales, revenue, conversion, return rate, customer acquisition cost. Regular cadence (daily in week 1, weekly thereafter).
- [ ] **Customer feedback synthesis** — Collect, organise, and analyse customer feedback from all channels: reviews, support emails, social media, direct conversations.
- [ ] **Issue triage & response** — Identify, prioritise, and resolve product or service issues. Classify by severity and frequency.
- [ ] **Inventory & reorder management** — Monitor stock levels, sales velocity, and trigger reorders with appropriate lead time.
- [ ] **V2 / iteration roadmap** — Collect and prioritise improvement ideas and feature requests for the next version or iteration.

### Conditional (select based on situation)

- [ ] **Review generation strategy** — Actively solicit reviews on key platforms. Respond to reviews. Build social proof.
- [ ] **Marketing iteration** — Optimise marketing based on early data: ad creative, targeting, messaging, channel mix.
- [ ] **Pricing optimisation** — Test and adjust pricing based on demand signals, competition, and margin data.
- [ ] **Channel expansion** — Evaluate and add new sales channels based on launch performance.
- [ ] **Retail account management** — Manage retail relationships: reorders, sell-through data, promotional support, new account outreach.
- [ ] **Quality monitoring** — Track defect rates, return reasons, warranty claims. Feed back to manufacturer.
- [ ] **Customer support setup** — Build FAQ, response templates, support processes as volume grows.
- [ ] **International expansion assessment** — Evaluate demand signals from other markets. Plan expansion if warranted.

### Optional / Deep Dive

- [ ] **Cohort analysis** — How do different customer groups behave over time? Repeat purchase, LTV, referral rates.
- [ ] **Product-market fit assessment** — Survey-based PMF measurement (e.g., Sean Ellis test: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?")
- [ ] **Competitor response monitoring** — How are competitors reacting to your launch? New products, price changes, messaging changes.
- [ ] **Expansion/line extension planning** — Based on market response, plan new SKUs, variants, bundles, or complementary products.

---

## Done Criteria

This phase doesn't "complete" in the traditional sense. Transition points include:

1. **Steady state** — the product is performing consistently and the operation is routine
2. **V2 decision** — enough data exists to make informed decisions about the next version
3. **Product sunset** — the product reaches end of life and is phased out
4. **New project entry** — learnings feed into Phase 00 of a new product development cycle

---

## Notes

- The first 30 days post-launch are the most information-rich period in the entire development process. Pay attention. Every signal matters.
- Resist the urge to over-react to early data. Wait for patterns, not individual data points. But also don't ignore consistent themes.
- The best V2 ideas come from this phase. Keep a running list and review it regularly.
- For solopreneurs: this phase is where operational reality hits. Customer service, inventory management, and ongoing marketing all compete for time. Build systems and routines early.
