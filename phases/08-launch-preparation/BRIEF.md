# Phase 08 — Launch Preparation: BRIEF

## Purpose

Prepare everything needed to bring the product to market. This phase answers: **"How do we reach customers, at what price, through which channels, and with what message?"**

Launch preparation often runs in parallel with Phases 06 and 07. Don't wait until the product is manufactured to start this work — the earlier you build audience and validate demand, the stronger the launch.

---

## Inputs Needed

- Phase 07 HANDOVER.md — availability date, unit cost, inventory, compliance status
- Phase 02 HANDOVER.md — channel research, pricing research, competitive positioning
- Phase 01 HANDOVER.md — user profile, jobs-to-be-done, emotional dimensions
- Brand/identity requirements from Phase 03
- Product images, samples, or renders for marketing content

---

## Sub-Task Menu

### Core (recommended for all projects)

- [ ] **Pricing strategy** — Final pricing: retail, wholesale, DTC. Margin analysis across channels. Promotional pricing plan.
- [ ] **Channel strategy** — Which channels to launch in, in what order. Requirements for each channel. Timeline.
- [ ] **Brand messaging & positioning** — Core value proposition, tagline, key messages, tone of voice. What to say and how.
- [ ] **Launch plan** — Timeline of launch activities. Phased rollout or big bang. Key milestones and dependencies.
- [ ] **Sales & marketing collateral** — What materials are needed: product photography, website, listings, sell sheets, social content.

### Conditional (select based on product type and channel)

- [ ] **E-commerce setup** — Own website, Shopify, Amazon listing, marketplace presence. Store design, product pages, checkout.
- [ ] **Retail sales preparation** — Sell sheets, line sheets, pricing sheets, buyer outreach, trade show planning, retail packaging requirements.
- [ ] **Crowdfunding campaign planning** — Platform selection, campaign page, reward tiers, video, stretch goals, fulfilment plan. If crowdfunding is also the funding mechanism (not just a channel), coordinate with Phase 07 funding execution — campaign timing, funding targets, and production triggers must align.
- [ ] **Investor/funder communications** — If externally funded: investor update materials, reporting cadence, milestone tracking against what was promised. Keep funders informed and maintain the relationship for potential follow-on support.
- [ ] **Social media & content strategy** — Platform selection, content calendar, content creation plan, community building.
- [ ] **PR & media outreach** — Press kit, media list, outreach strategy, review sample plan.
- [ ] **Influencer & community strategy** — Identify relevant influencers, partnership approach, seeding strategy.
- [ ] **Pre-launch / waitlist building** — Email capture, early access, pre-orders, audience building before launch.
- [ ] **Paid marketing strategy** — Ad platforms, budget, targeting, creative strategy, expected CAC.
- [ ] **Fulfilment & logistics setup** — Shipping rates, carrier selection, packaging for shipping, returns policy.
- [ ] **Legal preparation** — Terms of service, privacy policy, warranty terms, return policy, product liability insurance.

### Optional / Deep Dive

- [ ] **Launch event planning** — Physical or virtual launch event, demonstrations, media event.
- [ ] **Partnership & collaboration** — Co-marketing, bundle deals, complementary brands.
- [ ] **International launch planning** — Localisation, international shipping, regional compliance, multi-currency.
- [ ] **Post-launch marketing roadmap** — What happens after launch day? Sustained marketing plan for the first 90 days.

---

## Done Criteria

1. Pricing is set and margin is validated across all channels
2. At least one sales channel is ready to go live
3. Core marketing collateral is produced
4. Launch timeline is defined with clear milestones
5. Fulfilment and logistics can handle orders
6. Legal basics are covered
7. HANDOVER provides a launch execution checklist

---

## Notes

- For solopreneurs, the temptation is to delay launch preparation until the product is "ready." Resist this. Building audience and testing messaging can and should start during design and production.
- The channel strategy should be ruthlessly focused for launch. Start with 1-2 channels you can do well, not 5 you do poorly.
- Product photography is often underestimated. For physical products, great photos are the single highest-ROI marketing investment. Budget time and money for this.
- Pricing is a strategic decision, not just a cost-plus calculation. How you price positions the product in the market.
