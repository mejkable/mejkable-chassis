# Phase 00 — Opportunity Discovery: BRIEF

## Purpose

Identify and evaluate whether an opportunity is worth pursuing before committing resources. This phase answers: **"Is there a real opportunity here, and is it the right one for me/us?"**

Some projects enter the chassis with this already resolved (gut conviction, proven demand, commissioned work). If so, mark this phase `skipped` and document your reasoning in DECISIONS.md. The handover should still be completed with whatever is known.

---

## Inputs Needed

- An initial idea, observation, or problem space (can be rough)
- Any existing knowledge about the market or user
- Awareness of own capabilities, constraints, and goals

---

## Sub-Task Menu

Select the sub-tasks relevant to your project. Not all are required — pick what fits your product category and stage.

### Core (recommended for all projects)

- [ ] **Opportunity framing** — Articulate the opportunity in plain language. What have you noticed? Why might it matter?
- [ ] **Initial market scan** — Is there an existing market? How big roughly? Growing or shrinking? Who are the current players?
- [ ] **Target customer sketch** — Who would buy this? First-pass persona or customer segment description.
- [ ] **Founder-market fit check** — Why are you/your team the right people? What unfair advantages or relevant experience exist?

### Conditional (select based on product type and situation)

- [ ] **Trend & timing analysis** — Is there a cultural, technological, or regulatory tailwind? Why now?
- [ ] **Adjacent market scan** — Are there related markets or product categories that inform the opportunity?
- [ ] **Channel hypothesis** — First thinking on where this product would be sold (retail, DTC, wholesale, crowdfunding, etc.)
- [ ] **IP landscape scan** — Quick check on existing patents, trademarks, or protected designs in the space
- [ ] **Regulatory flag check** — Are there obvious regulatory hurdles (safety standards, certifications, age restrictions, import rules)?
- [ ] **Unit economics napkin math** — Very rough: what might it cost, what might it sell for, does the margin make sense?
- [ ] **Risk flags** — What could kill this? Capital requirements, technical risk, single points of failure.
- [ ] **Funding path assessment** — How will this be funded? Bootstrap, angel, VC, grants, crowdfunding, revenue from existing business? Rough capital requirements vs. available resources. Which funding paths are realistic and appropriate for this opportunity's scale and risk profile?

### Optional / Deep Dive

- [ ] **Analogous product study** — What succeeded or failed in a similar space, and what can be learned?
- [ ] **Customer signal gathering** — Any quick-and-dirty signal of demand (forums, social media, reviews of competitors, search trends)?
- [ ] **Strategic fit assessment** — How does this fit into a broader product line, brand, or business strategy?

---

## Done Criteria

This phase is complete when:

1. The opportunity is articulated clearly enough that someone unfamiliar could understand it
2. There's a first-pass assessment of market, customer, and viability
3. A go/no-go/pivot decision has been made and recorded
4. The HANDOVER.md is written with enough context for Phase 01 to begin

---

## Notes

- This is deliberately lightweight. The goal is to make a fast, informed decision — not to write a business plan.
- For projects entering with strong conviction, spend 30 minutes here anyway. The act of articulating the opportunity often reveals blind spots.
- Confidence rating of "Low" here is fine — that's expected at this stage. The question is whether it's promising enough to invest in deeper research.
